OpenTrails: Open Data for Our Public Lands

Written by

Alan Williams, OpenTrails Facilitator

Americans have inherited a vast and spectacular landscape. Through the efforts of citizens and governments throughout the 20th century, today Americans are the equal-owners of some 785 million acres of public land.

Working with a group of Code for America fellows, a coalition of parks in Northeast Ohio has developed an open data format for visitor information about trails and the public lands they traverse. With OpenTrails, the Coalition can speak with one voice to their 8 million annual visitors. OpenTrails data is designed to help them share their data with one another as well, offering a web and developer friendly format for use in apps like To The Trails and Transit & Trails, and platforms like Outer Spatial and CartoDB.

Today, Code for America is expanding this collaboration to include stewards anywhere. Literally thousands of the organizations care for our land, ranging from Federal agencies, state subdivisions, and cities to special districts, trusts, and non-profits. The "Open Trail System Specification" aims to empower them all to easily share open data between one another and the visiting public.
